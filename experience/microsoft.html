Overview

As a software engineering intern on Microsoft's Worldwide Learning team, I designed, built, and shipped a learner-engagement feature for the company's massive open online courses (MOOCs): a Kendo UI plugin that lets learners highlight and annotate video and text directly inside a course.

I drove the work end-to-end — from user interviews and design through a fully documented, well-tested implementation with collaborative annotation storage — and delivered it through agile sprints, reviewing and demoing the final solution to senior engineers and management.

What I worked on

  • Designed, developed, and demoed a Kendo UI video and text highlighting plugin for MOOCs, boosting content interaction and learner engagement.
  • Designed and implemented global and local databases that enabled collaborative user annotations, enriching the shared MOOC learning experience.
  • Ran user interviews to iteratively refine usability and let real learner feedback drive the design.
  • Delivered a fully documented solution with 95% unit-test coverage, reviewed and demoed to senior engineers and management.
  • Drove the work through agile sprints from planning to deployment.

Highlighted projects

Highlighting Plugin

Learners had no way to mark up course video and text as they studied. I designed and built a Kendo UI plugin that brings highlighting directly into the MOOC player, then demoed it to the team — making courses more interactive and lifting learner engagement.

Collaborative Annotations

Designed and implemented global and local databases so annotations could be both personal and shared, letting learners build on each other's highlights and notes to enrich the collective course experience.

Usability Research

Ran user interviews throughout development and fed the findings back into the design each sprint, refining usability so the feature matched how learners actually study.
